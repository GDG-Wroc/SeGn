# Problem Statement Canvas: Adaptive Building Climate Control

## 1. The Problem

Most modern buildings are constructed using static materials and insulation that are optimized for a single state or average condition[cite: 1]. Consequently, the same walls, roofs, and windows perform identically in January and July[cite: 1]. They cannot adapt dynamically as seasons change or as the daily weather fluctuates[cite: 1]. This static nature fails to maintain comfortable indoor temperatures efficiently throughout the year, creating a constant struggle against external environmental elements[cite: 1].

## 2. Who Has the Problem? (Stakeholders)

* **R&D Department (Client / Investor):** Needs a systematically engineered, verifiable, and transparent software/logic system that automates inventive problem-solving
* **Building Owners & Occupants:** Face high energy bills and thermal discomfort due to non-adaptive building envelopes
* **Global Community & Environmental Bodies:** Suffer from the massive carbon footprint and emissions generated by traditional heating and cooling systems

## 3. Why Is It Important? (Impact & SDGs)

* **Energy & Emissions:** Heating and cooling account for a major share of global energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions
* **UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):**
  * *SDG 13 (Climate Action):* Reducing reliance on fossil-fuel-powered temperature regulation is critical to curbing emissions
  * *SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities):* Making urban infrastructure resilient, energy-efficient, and sustainable
* **The AC Dilemma:** Air conditioning (AC) is a compounding failure; while it cools the inside, it expels massive amounts of heat into the environment, intensifying the urban heat island effect and driving up total energy demand

## 4. Current Alternatives & Limitations

* **Static Insulation (e.g., Fiberglass, Rockwool, Fixed Double/Triple Glazing):** Optimized for one season or a mediocre middle ground. It cannot shift properties between trapping heat in winter and shedding/reflecting heat in summer[cite: 1].
* **Active HVAC / AC Systems:** Highly energy-intensive, expensive, mechanically complex, and environmentally detrimental [heat expulsion, refrigerant leakage](cite: 1).

## 5. Constraints & Boundaries

* **No Heavy Energy Use:** Solutions must minimize or eliminate reliance on heavy external electricity grids[cite: 1].
* **AC Prohibition:** Traditional active Air Conditioning cannot be used as the primary solution due to its net-negative environmental heat generation[cite: 1].
* **Architecture & Logic Constraints:**
  * **Anti-Black-Box Rule:** The backend cannot be a single "black-box" LLM prompt masquerading as structure[cite: 1].
  * **Inspectability:** Every intermediate step (problem parsing, contradiction formulation, TRIZ matrix lookups, scoring) must run as a real, loggable, and distinct piece of program logic[cite: 1].

## 6. System Inputs & Outputs

* **System Input:** Unstructured text describing the inventive challenge [e.g., Problem 7 prompt](cite: 1).
* **System Output:** An inspectable, structured reasoning trail containing:
    1. Formulated technical contradiction[cite: 1].
    2. Exactly 3 TRIZ-derived candidates[cite: 1].
    3. Exactly 3 alternative-method-derived candidates[cite: 1].
    4. Multi-criteria evaluation matrix[cite: 1].
    5. Final chosen solution with logic justification[cite: 1].

## 7. Solution Criteria & Success Metrics

* **Algorithmic Breadth:** System must successfully process and generate at least 6 distinct candidate solutions using two separate methodologies [TRIZ Contradiction Matrix + 1 other method]
* **Traceability Score:** 100% of the final selections must be directly traceable back to the evaluation phase and the original problem constraints
* **Physical Feasibility:** Proposed physical solutions must target seasonal adaptability (dynamic thermal resistance or heat reflection) without active grid-heavy dependency
